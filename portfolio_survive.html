Survive Visit Website

Inspiration

When starting to live and study in a unfamiliar city, safety is always an important issue. As an international student in UQ, this sparked our idea of the educational website about teaching students how to deal with emergencies and offences and protect themselves.

The original idea of the website is to make it a story-based game, in this way, users can learn safety knowledges in a more engaging way. With the offences rate dataset to keep students to stay alert. And the location & contact of nearby police stations, hospitals, and ambulance stations are extracted from datasets respectively and allow users to get familiar with their living surroundings. In this stage, I searched for relative informations and dataset that might be useful, came up with potential ideas of constructing the website, and discussed how could the game suitable for target users.

Design

In the proposal process, We spent large amount of time designing the game as it is the most important feature of the website. As the target users are new university students, the game should be interactive, and the content should be intuitive and contains sufficient safety knowledge. With this consideration, we designed the game as a linear text-based story game with real situation and location, and the story is pushed forward by different choices for users in each plot. Because we realized that same situation happens in different place could have diverse options to handle, such as considering the distance to the police station or hospital to decide how to seek help when facing an offence. This aspect was designed as a real map for users to view current location and nearby services.

We discussed different interactions in the game, such as the whole game and choices based on the map, or text-based descriptions, etc. Because interactions on the map could be inconspicuous and can be confused, we decides to make plots with text descriptions, image illustration, and map supports. Users make choices by clicking the text, and different options could have diverse consequences and visualizing evaluations according to some authorized articles and related laws.

As a decisive part of the process, I looked for various game-related or narrative website for inspiration and reference, designed some main interactions supported by sketches , and proposed some additional features in the website. Although not all of them are adopted in the final product, but it helped us to make the best option for our design.

Prototyping

The original color theme in our prototype was light blue with low contrast. But according to some of the user feedbacks, some important features are inconspicuous and sometimes they didn't notice them. In this case, we decided to use some bright colors. With an idea of retro style and a feeling of old notebook and detective, we tried the color theme of orange-yellow-brown. Still, it looks wierd especially with the real map embedded, and it doesn't feels serious enough as we named it "Survive". Finally, we decide to make it black-white-grey themed, because the topic of safety is about life and death, the high contrast of black and white can present a sense of solemn and warn users to take it seriously. With key elements such as current choices and services link set to light orange, it could guide users with the whole process and provide a sense of safety and hope just like the meaning of orange.

As for the layout, it is hard for us to balance the scene image and the map in the same page. When users enter the page, the first thing they see should be the most important feature, which is the scene image because it is intuitive. Then the layout should be easy to follow, that is read about the scene and look at the options and the map. With this in mind, we adopted a left/right layout with the scene image takes the largest place. Descriptions and choices are on the right for users to read through, and the map takes place at a corner for users to refer. Although we have considered about a up/down layout which could be easier to follow, but it asymmetric looking makes it lacks aethetics, so this propose was abandoned.

The game map page is the entrance of different games, users choose different city and they can see its detailed offences data and start the game. Originally it was designed as a real map of QLD with icons represent cities as the game entrance. But some user feedback about our prototype mentioned that there are too many elements on this page, this might lead to information overload. And also the color theme is not compatible as customization is not supported for Leaflet.js. In consideration of simplify this page and with inspirations from other narrative websites, the map was replaced by a concise and abstract one with outline and several main road of QLD visible only. In this case, information can be easily recognised and pins of the city are more conspicuous.

We made most of our changes of the design in the prototype & testing process. As I mainly made the website prototype with resources created by team members, generated several potential layout and colors in the prototype for team deciding, and produced the simplified game entrance map from external sources, it reflected the importance of discussing with team members because sometimes they can realize something that I can't, and come up with better ideas.

Implementation

To build the website, a MySQL database is used for the storage of game plots and their sequences. 3 tables were designed for whole story, scene, and choice data respectively, and these data was loaded after each choice made in each scene according to a specific sequence in it. Additional informations such as scene images, choice consequences, different avatars, etc. is also stored in extra fields in these table to present a various experience in different game story.

Besides, dataset were used both within and outside the game feature. At the description of the game entrance, data of LGA offences rate are presented to users with a compirason to other cities to provide a background information and also inform users to be cautious. Services location data including police stations, hospital, fire stations, and ambulance stations are presented on the in-game map as reference marks. While "Services" page is where users can specifically view and search any kind of nearby services mentioned above, and the map with locations of these services is the main element on this page, and additional text informations such as address and telephone are extracted from dataset and present on the list as well to provide users with details. For those who don't have phone numbers in dataset, a general number of 000 was displayed instead.

For the implementation process, I led the construction of the website, designed and set up the MySQL database and connected with the website, implemented most of the challenging functions including interactions, animations, data transfering, etc., and finding finding external resources such as icons and fonts. As we got some feedback from tutors in the last few weeks, I also made some improvements of some features according to these feedbacks after discussed with team members, such as the star-rating and font size issues. Although the final product still have some problems and potential improvements to be made, generally the current website can meet the need of safety education we have proposed at the beginning.

Reflections

For the project, we have made a functional website that provides with essensial safety knowledge for students. The user test session and feedbacks from the teaching team have provided us ideas of what users would care and what we need to concern as a designer. Although there are still some problems to be solved and some potential improvement could be made according to what we have learned from the final tradeshow, in general, we believe that the whole process and outcomes are successful, and also recognized that a good functionality is important than some cool visual effects. However, regardless some limitations of this course and technical issues, the website could have more features. Such as adjusting map location according to users' location so university students from all over the world can use it. And the game could be more interesting if the story is designed as branched instead of linear, so users can have completely different storylines made by themselves. Besides, more additional information could be used to help users make the right choices, because they might still feel unreliable even with some tips after each scene.

For this course, it provides me a good practical opportunity of designing and team working. As I have been worked as a developer, I didn't think about the design and its rationality. During the work in this studio, I took part in the project as a designer as well, and have more aspects to be concern rather than just coding. It also helps me a lot as have theoretical knowledge from previous courses utilized in this project such as heuristic principles and Fitts' law. Besides, it would be better if the course project could be more like a real project in industry. Because these projects usually have specific backgrounds and limitations, so this might focus more on the depth rathe than the breadth of design. Moreover, although all team members are encouraged to take part in the design and coding work, but some times the division of work varies, and as students from master of IT and master of Interaction Design can form a team together, so if an interaction design student did more coding work than IT students, this could lead to a mismatch of their major and lack improvement of their corresponding skills.
